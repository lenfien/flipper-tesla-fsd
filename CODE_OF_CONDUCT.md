# Code of Conduct

## The short version

Be civil. Assume good faith. Don't be a jerk. If you can't say it to
someone's face without starting a fight, don't type it here either.

## The less short version

This project has contributors and users from a lot of countries who
don't share a first language. People are going to ask questions that
seem obvious, file issues that are duplicates, test on firmware that
broke things, and occasionally get details wrong. That's normal. We
answer patiently, point to the relevant doc, and move on.

What we expect:

- **Be patient with language.** English is most people's second or
  third language here. If you don't understand a question, ask for
  clarification rather than dismissing it.
- **Be specific with bug reports and test results.** "It doesn't work"
  is not actionable; "HW4 Model 3 on 2026.8.6, Auto Detect, RX=0 after
  10s, no 0x398 in stock CAN sniffer either" is.
- **Credit prior work.** We ported from CanFeather, we use opendbc, we
  borrow from mikegapinski and tuncasoftbildik. If your contribution
  derives from someone else's research, say so.
- **Keep discussions about the software.** Not about each other.

What we won't tolerate:

- **Personal attacks** on other contributors, users, or project maintainers.
- **Harassment** — sustained disruption, unwanted personal contact,
  doxxing, or any of the usual categories.
- **Scammy behaviour** — promoting paid "unlockers" that re-package
  this GPL-licensed work without credit, fake test reports to boost a
  sales pitch, etc.
- **Safety-reckless requests** — asking the maintainer to add features
  that affect braking, steering, airbags, or any other safety-critical
  vehicle system. We will decline and close the thread.
- **Spam** in issues, PRs, discussions, or commit comments.

## Reporting

If something feels off and you don't want to raise it publicly, email
hypery11@gmail.com with the subject `[conduct]`. Reports are read by
the maintainer and not shared.

For anything involving physical safety of a vehicle, mail subject
`[security]` and see [`SECURITY.md`](SECURITY.md).

## Scope

This covers everything in this repository: issues, pull requests, the
wiki, discussions, commit messages, and any project spaces we set up
in the future (Discord, mailing list, etc.).
